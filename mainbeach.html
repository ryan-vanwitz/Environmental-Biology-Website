Main Beach

Main Beach in Crystal Lake, Illinois, boasts an enchanting ecosystem that harmoniously blends natural beauty with recreational spaces. The beach's pristine surroundings include flourishing flora and fauna, creating a serene environment for visitors. From the tranquil waters of Crystal Lake to the vibrant shoreline, Main Beach stands as a captivating destination, offering a delightful retreat where the stunning ecosystem enhances the overall recreational experience for all who enjoy its shores.

Positive Attributes of Main Beach

  • Pristine Sandy Shorelines
  • Biodiversity of Aquatic Life
  • Well-Preserved Natural Habitat
  • Beautiful Surrounding Landscape
  • Recreational Opportunities for Visitors
  • Community Gathering Space
  • Sustainable Environmental Practices

Potential Environmental Issues

  • There are envasive species in the lake and at the beach with the most apparent species being the zebra mussel.
  • Water pollution into the lake via the driving of motorized boats with the gas and oil pollution.
  • Water pollution into the lake via runoff of residental use of fertilizers.
  • Due to the lack of movement in the lake, bacteria can reproduce and spread in the form of "swimmer's itch".

Optimized Solutions

  • In order to slowly reduce the zebra mussel population, I would introduce more of their natural predators to the ecosystem (i.e. specific fish and birds).
  • To reduce the water pollution from motorized boats, I would regulate the amount of permits given for the lake, so the exposure to gas/oil is reduced.
  • To reduce the water pollution from fertilizers, I would monetarily incentivize surronding property owners not to use fertilizers.
  • To reduce the bacteria build-ups during the summer months, I would add aeration systems or pumps to the lake for forced movement of the stagnant water which should prevent the collection of bacteria.
