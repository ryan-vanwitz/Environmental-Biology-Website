Veterans Acres Park

Veterans Acres Park in Crystal Lake, Illinois, boasts an extraordinary ecosystem that captivates visitors with its diverse natural wonders. The park features scenic landscapes, tranquil walking trails, and lush greenery, providing a habitat for a rich variety of wildlife. From vibrant bird species to native plant life, Veterans Acres Park stands as a remarkable haven of biodiversity, offering an immersive experience in the heart of nature for all who explore its enchanting surroundings.

Positive Attributes of Veterans Acres Park

  • Scenic Natural Beauty
  • Tranquil Walking Trails
  • Diverse Wildlife Observation
  • Well-Maintained Recreational Areas
  • Family-Friendly Atmosphere
  • Outdoor Recreation Opportunities

Potential Environmental Issues

  • There are many envasive plant species.
    • Buckthorn grows rapidly and sufficates native plant species from ever repopulating.
    • Duckweed covers the top layer of the pond which adds to the eutrophication of the water.
  • Habitat fragmentation occurs with the large telephone towers streaking through the middle of the park.
  • Human pollution via littering due to the lack of trash cans in the back areas of the extensive trails.

Optimized Solutions

  • In order to erdicate the buckthorn, I would work to cut down the envasive species and spot-treat it with a pesticide, so only that stump gets the pesticide.
  • In order to minimzie the eutrophication of the pond, I would add an aeration system so at least the native plants and fish in the pond recieve the necesary oxygen to survive.
  • The large telephone towers can either be switched to an underground system so forest and wildlife may return to that area or they can be rerouted around the park to eliminate the habitat fragmentation.
  • To reduce the littering of garbage and cans, more trash cans can be placed throughout the extensive park trails.
